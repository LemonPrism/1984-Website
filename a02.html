NEWSPEAK UPDATE

Ministry of Truth Bulletin No. 607

Approved Newspeak Vocabulary for Loyal Citizens

The Ministry of Truth is pleased to remind all citizens that the continued development of Newspeak represents one of the Party's greatest achievements. As Comrade Syme correctly observed, the purpose of Newspeak is to "narrow the range of thought" (55). Citizens are therefore encouraged to eliminate unnecessary and outdated Oldspeak words from their daily vocabulary.

Beginning immediately, the following substitutions are strongly recommended:

Oldspeak Approved Newspeak
Excellent Plusgood
Wonderful Doubleplusgood
Bad Ungood
Very bad Doubleplusungood
Freedom [Word removed]
Democracy [Word removed]
Justice Goodthink
Rebellion Thoughtcrime
Individuality Ownlife

Citizens should note that many Oldspeak words are no longer necessary. For example, rather than maintaining separate words such as "splendid," "excellent," "magnificent," and "outstanding," the word good may be modified into plusgood or doubleplusgood. This efficient system reduces confusion and promotes ideological clarity.

The Research Department further reminds citizens that "every year fewer and fewer words" are required, resulting in "the range of consciousness always a little smaller" (55). Such progress demonstrates the scientific superiority of Ingsoc linguistics.

Special attention should be paid to words concerning political concepts. Citizens have occasionally reported confusion regarding terms such as liberty, personal rights, independent thought, and freedom of expression. The Party is pleased to announce that these terms have become increasingly obsolete. As language continues to improve, many such concepts will no longer require expression.

Furthermore, all writers, journalists, teachers, and public speakers are instructed to avoid excessive vocabulary. Excessive vocabulary creates opportunities for ambiguity, and ambiguity creates opportunities for misunderstanding. The ideal sentence is clear, efficient, and incapable of being interpreted in an unorthodox manner.

The Ministry proudly reports that the upcoming Twelfth Edition of the Newspeak Dictionary will contain significantly fewer entries than previous editions. This achievement represents another step toward linguistic perfection. As Comrade Syme predicted, "The Revolution will be complete when the language is perfect" (55).

Citizens are therefore encouraged to embrace the future. Speak less. Think less. Question less. In doing so, you will become more capable of fulfilling your duty to Big Brother and the Party.
